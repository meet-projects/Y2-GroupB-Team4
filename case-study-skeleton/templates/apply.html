Application Process

Applications to GHIS may be submitted from October 15th until

July 1st. Please be aware that we have limited spaces and

decisions are made on a first-come, first-served basis. Therefore

early application is highly recommended.


PDP - 10th Grade Candidates

Imporatant Note: PDP class is currently open for local students only.

1. Create an account in Open Apply, save your login information and complete the online application form.

2. Upload your most recent school grade report (an English translation must be provided), a legible copy of the first two pages of your passport, and a passport-sized photo. A $30 registration fee (non-refundable) must be paid in order to process your application.

3. Candidates living in Israel will be contacted by email to attend a screening workshop on campus. The workshop will include placement tests.

4. A personal essay assignment will be sent to you via email. You should complete this assignment within a week and then upload it together with your two letters of recommendation from an academic teacher at your current school and an adult who knows you through your extra-curricular activities.

5. Candidates who passed stages 1-3, will be invited for an interview with the GHIS Director of Admissions.

6. Second interviews and/or placement tests may be required. Students are accepted on the basis of their academic achievements, English level, leadership potential, and enthusiasm.

7. Allow four weeks to receive a final decision from the school.

PDP - 11th Grade Candidates

1. Create an account in Open Apply, save your login information and complete the online application form.

2. Upload your most recent school grade report (an English translation must be provided), a legible copy of the first two pages of your passport, and a passport-sized photo. A $30 registration fee (non-refundable) must be paid in order to process your application.

3. You will be contacted by email to schedule a Zoom interview with the GHIS Director of Admissions. The interview will be conducted in English and take around 45 minutes.

4. A personal essay assignment will be sent to you via email. You should complete this assignment within a week and then upload it together with your two letters of recommendation from an academic teacher at your current school and an adult who knows you through your extra-curricular activities.

5. If you pass stages 2-4, you will be invited to an online screening workshop. The workshop will take 1.5 hours and will include teamwork assignments and discussions with candidates from around the world.

6. Second interviews and/or placement tests may be required. Students are accepted on the basis of their academic achievements, English level, leadership potential, and enthusiasm.

7. Allow four weeks to receive a final decision from the school.
